About Me

I am a Data Science enthusiast with a strong foundation in pharmacology, public health, and healthcare program implementation. My academic and professional experiences have shown me how critical high-quality data and timely insights are to improving health outcomes, shaping effective policy, and optimizing healthcare delivery, particularly in low-resource settings.

My work in HIV/AIDS programs and health data monitoring has strengthened my commitment to advancing Good Health and Well-Being by addressing gaps in data-driven decision-making across healthcare systems.

Mission & Focus

I am deeply aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3: Good Health and Well-Being. My passion lies in leveraging data science to improve access, efficiency, and equity in healthcare delivery, especially for underserved and low-resource communities.

Through health analytics, predictive modeling, and evidence-based decision-making, I aim to support better clinical, operational, and policy decisions that lead to measurable improvements in public health outcomes.
